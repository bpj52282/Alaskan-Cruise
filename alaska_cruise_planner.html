Key packing insight: You'll experience a ~43°F swing across this trip — from a hot Carolina summer averaging 91°F to near-50°F at the Dawes Glacier at 6:30 AM on July 8. The smartest strategy is packable layers: thermal base → fleece mid-layer → waterproof shell. Good to Know — Radiance Class Tips
💡
Radiance-Class Insider Tips
Especially relevant for Alaska sailings
  • Solarium is adults-only — the enclosed heated pool on Deck 11 is ideal for Alaska's cold, cloudy weather. Stake out a chair early; it fills up.
  • Skip Windjammer at 8 AM — breakfast rush peaks 7:30–9:30 AM on sea days. Arrive at 7:00 or after 9:30 to avoid the lines.
  • Pool chairs vanish on sea days — towels claim chairs before 8 AM. The Solarium is a better uncrowded alternative.
  • Viking Crown Lounge (Deck 13) — panoramic wrap-around windows make this the best spot on the ship for glacier and fjord views. Arrive early on July 8.
  • Top decks are windy at speed — Radiance-class ships run noticeably windier on open upper decks while underway. Pack a jacket even on sunny days.

eMuster Safety Drill
🛟
Muster Drill — New Process
Must be completed before the ship departs Vancouver on July 5
1
Watch the safety video in the Royal Caribbean app or on your stateroom TV after boarding
2
Go to your assigned muster station and check in with a crew member — takes about 2 minutes
3
App marks you complete — no group assembly or long wait on the pool deck
Your muster station is printed on your SeaPass card. The ship cannot depart until all guests check in, so aim to complete this by 3:30 PM. This replaces the old all-hands deck assembly — no more waiting in the sun.
